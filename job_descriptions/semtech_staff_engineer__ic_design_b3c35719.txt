Analog Engineer 3

Position Summary: The Analog Engineer 3 position is responsible for leading analog circuit engineering functions for significant product areas

within our semiconductor company. This position develops analog architecture strategies, manages complex circuit implementations, and provides expert

guidance on analog technology and optimization. The role requires advanced expertise in analog design, strong analytical abilities, and leadership skills

to ensure excellent circuit performance while supporting business objectives in the highly technical semiconductor design environment.

Key Responsibilities

Analog Architecture and Design Leadership (25%):



Develop and implement analog architectures and design strategies for significant product lines or technology platforms
Create comprehensive analog specifications, performance requirements, and design methodologies
Design innovative circuit solutions to address specialized semiconductor application challenges
Establish performance objectives and technical metrics
Align analog designs with product objectives and market requirements Advanced Analog Circuit Design (20%):
Lead complex analog circuit and subsystem design initiatives
Develop sophisticated analog blocks, precision references, and interface circuits
Implement advanced circuit techniques for performance optimization
Create reference designs and architectural templates
Drive design excellence through innovative topologies and techniques Analog Integration and System Optimization (15%):
Lead analog-digital integration and system optimization
Develop interface strategies and noise management approaches
Create system performance models and circuit budgets
Implement power optimization and area efficiency techniques
Ensure optimal system performance through comprehensive integration approaches


Design for Manufacturability and Yield (15%):



Lead design for manufacturability initiatives for analog circuits
Develop sophisticated approaches for process variation tolerance and yield optimization
Create process-portable design methodologies and migration strategies
Implement advanced techniques for matching, layout, and parasitic management
Ensure robust designs across process corners, voltage, and temperature Technical Problem Solving and Innovation (15%):
Lead resolution of complex analog technical challenges
Develop advanced troubleshooting methodologies and debug strategies
Create innovative solutions for difficult analog performance issues
Implement novel techniques for circuit optimization and enhancement
Drive continuous improvement through technical excellence and innovation Technical Leadership and Collaboration (10%):
Serve as analog subject matter expert to product and system teams
Lead cross-functional analog initiatives and development projects
Provide technical mentoring to engineering teams
Build collaborative relationships with system architects, digital designers, and platform teams
Represent analog expertise in technical forums and reviews


Education And Experience



Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering or related technical field; Master's degree preferred
5-7 years of progressive experience in analog


circuit design and semiconductor engineering



Experience leading analog design projects and complex circuit implementations
Advanced knowledge of analog design techniques, device physics, and circuit topologies
Experience in semiconductor industry required


Qualifications



Expert knowledge of analog circuit design principles, methodologies, and techniques
Advanced understanding of semiconductor device physics, analog


layout, and process technology impacts



Strong knowledge of various analog circuit blocks (amplifiers, references, data converters, regulators, etc.)
Experience with analog simulation tools, design verification, and characterization methods
Excellent project leadership capabilities with experience


managing complex design initiatives



Strong analytical and problem-solving skills
Superior technical communication and documentation abilities
Knowledge of semiconductor manufacturing processes and analog design constraints
Strategic thinking with ability to align analog approaches with product objectives
Experience with mixed-signal design, power management, and system integration
Understanding of reliability mechanisms, qualification, and test methodologies


Travel Requirements



Moderate travel required (10-15% of time)
May include travel between design centers, manufacturing facilities, and customer sites


Level Of Authority



Leads analog engineering for significant product areas or technology platforms
Makes decisions regarding analog architectures, design approaches,


and circuit implementations



Provides authoritative guidance on analog matters and methodology applications
Approves analog specifications and design approaches
May supervise analog engineers or coordinate cross-functional design teams


Job Complexity



Performs highly complex analog engineering activities requiring advanced expertise and judgment
Addresses sophisticated analog challenges involving


multiple variables and performance dimensions



Develops circuit solutions with significant product performance and market implications
Requires deep understanding of semiconductor physics, device behavior, and process technology
Expected to balance performance, power efficiency, area, and reliability objectives
Navigates complex technical tradeoffs and competing requirements
Works in highly specialized semiconductor environment requiring innovative analog approaches
