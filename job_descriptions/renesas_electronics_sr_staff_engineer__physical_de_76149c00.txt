Job Description



We are seeking a Principal Engineer – Physical Design to lead implementation and optimization of low-power, chiplet-based MCU designs on cost-optimized mature process nodes. This role focuses on delivering high-quality, affordable microcontrollers by leveraging proven technologies and efficient design methodologies.



Job Description



Own end-to-end physical design for chiplet-based MCU SoCs, including floorplanning, placement, power planning, Signal integrity, routing, timing closure and physical verification
Apply best practices for mature-node design to achieve cost efficiency without compromising performance
Collaborate with architecture and packaging teams to enable seamless chiplet connectivity and inter-die interfaces.
Ensure physical integrity and manufacturability for multi-die systems using established packaging technologies
Implement low-power techniques such as clock gating, power gating, and leakage reduction tailored for mature nodes
Balance power, timing, and area trade-offs to meet stringent cost and efficiency targets
Work closely with RTL, verification, and DFT teams to ensure design convergence
Partner with packaging and test engineering for robust product delivery
Define best practices for physical design flows optimized for mature nodes and chiplet architectures
Evaluate tools and flows that enhance productivity and cost-effectiveness


Qualifications


BTech/MTech in Electrical/Electronic Engineering, Computer Engineering or Computer Science with 10+ years of experience in ASIC/SoC physical design with strong focus on low-power and cost-optimized designs
Proven experience in mature-node technologies (e.g., 12nm, 22nm) and chiplet-based architectures
Expertise in Cadence/Synopsys P&R tools and sign-off flows (STA, IR-drop, EM analysis)
Extensive experience dealing with power intent (UPF/CPF) and multi-voltage design
Excellent leadership and communication skills
Experience with AI/ML based flow development and/or physical implementation is desired
Experience with packaging standards for chiplet integration is a plus
