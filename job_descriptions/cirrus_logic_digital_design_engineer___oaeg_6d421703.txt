For over four decades, Cirrus Logic has been propelled by the top engineers in mixed-signal processing. Our rockstar team thrives on solving complex challenges with innovative end-user solutions for the world's top consumer brands. Cirrus Logic is also known for its award-winning culture, built on a foundation of inclusion and fairness, meaningful community engagement, and delivering enjoyable employee experiences at every turn. But we couldn’t do it without our extraordinary workforce – and that’s where you come in. Join our team and help us continue to make Cirrus Logic an exceptional place to grow your career!

We have an exciting opportunity for a talented Digital Design Engineer to join our growing design and development team in Edinburgh. In this role, you will have the opportunity to develop industry leading mixed-signal SOCs for consumer markets and become part of an organisation that operates at the forefront of cutting-edge technology. We offer an incredible work culture where individual value is key, along with personal/professional development dictated by you.

Responsibilities:


Design and develop new digital circuit architectures on advanced process nodes.
Work with systems, analog, physical design and verification engineers to ensure a fully specified, verified and correctly implemented design.
Contribute to product definition, prototype evaluation, test development and product release to ensure that the ASIC can be manufactured, is competitive and meets the customers’ requirements.
Participate in project meetings and design reviews.


Required Skills and Qualifications:


Bachelor’s, Master's or PhD degree in Electrical Engineering (or related discipline)
Comprehensive understanding of digital circuit design in RTL using Verilog and SystemVerilog
Knowledge and understanding of verification of block-level digital designs
Experience and understanding of synthesis and timing analysis of digital circuits
Knowledge of design for testing and manufacturing
Understanding and knowledge of high-volume manufacturing processes
An innovative, creative, lateral thinking problem solver
Excellent attention to detail and ability to interpret results
Strong diagnostic and analytical skills
Strong ability to relay complex information and collaborate with diverse teams using high level communication skills


This position is based in our Edinburgh office. It is a hybrid role with the flexibility to work from home, depending on business needs. Candidates must live within a commutable distance or be willing to relocate.
