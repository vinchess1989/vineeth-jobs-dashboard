Server CPU Physical Design Engineer - ASIC / CPU Design / Physical Implementation / Semiconductor




We are currently partnered with a leading semiconductor technology company developing next-generation high-performance server processors for advanced computing applications. As part of their continued growth, they are looking to hire a Server CPU Physical Design Engineer to contribute to the implementation and optimisation of cutting-edge CPU designs from RTL through to GDSII.




Key responsibilities

Own or contribute to CPU block implementation from RTL/netlist through to GDSII, including synthesis, floorplanning, power planning, placement, clock tree synthesis (CTS), routing, optimisation, ECO implementation, and sign-off.
Drive timing closure across multiple operating modes, process corners, and performance conditions.
Deliver high-performance, low-power CPU designs that meet aggressive power, performance, and area (PPA) targets.
Debug and resolve complex implementation challenges relating to timing, congestion, clocking, routing, IR drop, power integrity, electromigration (EM), ECO closure, DRC/LVS, and physical verification.
Collaborate closely with architecture, RTL, circuit design, CAD, SoC, verification, and post-silicon teams to optimise implementation quality and product performance.




Key requirements

Strong commercial experience in ASIC or CPU physical design implementation.
Proven expertise in synthesis, floorplanning, placement, clock tree synthesis (CTS), routing, timing closure, ECO implementation, and physical sign-off.
Strong understanding of static timing analysis (STA) and timing closure methodologies across complex semiconductor designs.
Experience balancing timing, power, area, congestion, and routability to achieve optimal PPA.
Knowledge of high-performance and low-power physical implementation techniques.
Hands-on experience with industry-standard EDA tools such as Genus, Innovus, Fusion Compiler, PrimeTime, Tempus, Voltus, RedHawk, Conformal, or equivalent.




Keywords
