The Product Engineering Manager will lead the product engineering function for power and high‑power semiconductor devices, with a strong focus on device characterization, qualification, release‑to‑production, and yield optimization. This role requires deep expertise in power semiconductor technology and hands‑on experience managing qualification programs for high‑voltage/high‑current devices. The manager will collaborate closely with technology, design, reliability, quality, and operations teams to ensure robust, reliable products that meet global standards and customer expectations.




Key Responsibilities

Product Qualification & Characterization

Own the qualification strategy, planning, and execution for power and high‑power semiconductor devices.
Lead characterization of new product performance before manufacturing release, including electrical, thermal, and reliability assessments
Ensure compliance with global standards (e.g., JEDEC, IEC, IEEE) and customer‑specific qualification requirements
Manage RMA / field return analysis in collaboration with quality and reliability teams

Product Engineering Leadership

Lead and mentor the product engineering team focused on power semiconductor device lifecycle activities, including NPI, qualification, ramp‑up, and sustenance
Develop and maintain engineering databases, test flows, and documentation frameworks
Oversee wafer sort and final test data disposition, yield enhancement initiatives, and statistical process control implementation.

Cross‑Functional Collaboration

Collaborate with device design, technology development, packaging, and manufacturing to ensure seamless release into production
Act as technical liaison between engineering, operations, quality, and customer engineering teams.
Support New Product Introduction (NPI) and ensure smooth transition from engineering builds to volume manufacturing

Reliability & Failure Analysis

Work with reliability teams to define, execute, and review reliability stress tests (HTRB, H3TRB, TC, HTOL, power cycling).
Drive root‑cause investigations for failures during qualification or production using FA labs, DOE methodologies, and Six Sigma‑aligned practices

Continuous Improvement

Implement data‑driven methods to optimize yield, improve device robustness, and reduce cost.
Monitor product performance trends and drive corrective/improvement actions.
Evaluate new tools, methodologies, and workflows to strengthen the product engineering function.




Required Qualifications

Bachelor’s or Master’s degree in Electrical Engineering, Electronics, Semiconductor Device Physics, or related fields.
Minimum 8–12 years experience in power semiconductor product engineering, including device characterization, qualification, and release‑to‑production
Knowledge of power semiconductor device physics
Proven experience in device qualification methodologies, reliability standards, and statistical analysis of power semiconductor performance
Hands‑on experience with test methodologies, power electronics measurements, and debugging of high‑power devices.




Preferred Skills

Experience working with wide‑bandgap devices (SiC, GaN)
Familiarity with yield enhancement and Six‑Sigma‑based problem solving
Experience with wafer fabrication, packaging processes, and final test flows for power devices.
Strong knowledge of SPC, DOE, reliability physics of failure, and qualification road‑mapping.




Soft Skills

Strong leadership capabilities with demonstrated ability to mentor engineering teams.
Excellent communication skills for cross‑global stakeholder engagement.
Ability to operate in a fast‑paced environment, managing multiple programs simultaneously.
