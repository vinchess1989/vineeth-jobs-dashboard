Job Description

High-Speed Interface Characterization

About The Company

Microchip FPGA Product Group is the leading supplier of nonvolatile, low-power programmable technologies. Our mission is to manage power consumption at both the chip and the system level, leveraging the industry's lowest power FPGAs and unique mixed-signal FPGAs to offer system designers a competitive edge. Our Design Center in Hyderabad, India is a growing and vital member of the group.

About This Position

You will join the silicon characterization team to execute characterization of FPGA products. You will work closely with silicon design engineers, software engineers and technology development engineers to ensure that the company deploys new products with the highest quality and shortest time to market. This position will provide the opportunity to work on multiple projects supporting key functions within the organization. Based on the relevant years of experience and exposure, this position can provide opportunities to lead or manage a team.

Primary Job Responsibilities


Technically lead the team in the area of FPGA characterization with emphasis on high-speed protocols
Develop Characterization spec, plan, and documentation
Characterization of Digital and Mixed-signal designs
Associated design and firmware development
Verification of Microchip’s FPGA timing and Power analysis tools
Correlation of pre-silicon results with silicon measurements
Cross-functional team interfacing and collaboration in all activities


Requirements/Qualifications

Required Skills and Experience:


BSEE or MSEE
12+ years of experience in product characterization or silicon validation with emphasis on digital and mixed-signal circuits
5+ years of experience in characterization and/or validation of serial protocols (PCIe/10GBaseKR/XAUI/SATA/etc.)
Expertise in characterization of DDR, SoC, processors, PLL, Oscillators, etc.
Firmware Development on multi-core microcontrollers or processors (ARM/RISC-V or similar)
Experience working in a laboratory environment, using lab equipment such as BERTs, logic analyzers, spectrum analyzers, oscilloscopes, function generators, etc.
Experience in evaluation of various laboratory equipment such as BERTs, Oscilloscopes, etc.
In-depth knowledge of electrical engineering fundamentals including CMOS device operation and characteristics
Experience in board design fundamentals, signal and power integrity
Experience with Verilog or VHDL
Exposure to automated characterization flows to maximize use of equipment
Initiative (self-motivated, self-confident, self-driven, self-learning, always striving for excellence)
Commitment (responsible, responsive, commit to winning)
Solid functional and technical skills
Work quality ("do it right the first time", attention to detail, good documentation)
Strong analytical and problem-solving skills
Excellent communication, documentation and presentation skills
Must have strong self-learning ability, leadership, and enjoy working in teams


Preferred Skills


Knowledge of JTAG protocol highly desirable
Working knowledge of SOC and/or FPGA Architectures
Knowledge of FPGA design synthesis and routing associated with Microchip (Libero), AMD/Xilinx (Vivado, ISE), or Intel/Altera (Quartus) tools
Working knowledge of at least 1 of the following languages: Java/ C/C++, Visual Basic and/or Perl
Analog and mixed signal circuit design. Working experience with analog and/or digital systems is an advantage


Travel Time

0% - 25%
