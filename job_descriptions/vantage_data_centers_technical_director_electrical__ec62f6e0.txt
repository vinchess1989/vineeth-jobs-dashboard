About Vantage Data Centers

Vantage Data Centers powers, cools, protects and connects the technology of the world’s well-known hyperscalers, cloud providers and large enterprises. Developing and operating across North America, EMEA and Asia Pacific, Vantage has evolved data center design in innovative ways to deliver dramatic gains in reliability, efficiency and sustainability in flexible environments that can scale as quickly as the market demands.

About Vantage Data Centers

Vantage Data Centers designs, builds, and operates mission-critical digital infrastructure for the world’s leading hyperscalers, cloud providers, and AI platforms. Operating across North America, EMEA, and APAC, Vantage delivers scalable, high-performance data center solutions with a focus on reliability, speed to market, operational resilience, and long-term asset value.

As demand for AI-ready infrastructure, high-density compute environments, and resilient digital platforms accelerates across EMEA, Vantage continues to expand and evolve its engineering capability to support increasingly complex mission-critical infrastructure systems.

The Mission Critical Engineering (MCE) team acts as the technical authority within the CTO function, responsible for engineering assurance, technical governance, reference design integrity, resilience validation, engineering leadership across major programmes, and engineering excellence throughout the EMEA portfolio.

Position Overview

The Technical Director, Electrical Engineering – Mission Critical Engineering, EMEA is a senior engineering leadership role within the CTO organisation, responsible for technical authority, engineering excellence, electrical design governance, leadership of electrical engineering teams across complex mission-critical data centre programmes, and engineering assurance across Vantage’s EMEA data centre portfolio.

Reporting directly to the Vice President, Mission Critical Engineering, EMEA, this role provides technical leadership across complex mission-critical infrastructure projects spanning hyperscale, colocation, AI, and high-density compute deployments.

The role acts as a senior subject matter expert (SME), and a technical lead on DC projects, for electrical engineering systems across the full project lifecycle, from concept planning and reference design through detailed engineering, procurement, construction, commissioning, operational readiness, and technical optimisation.

This is not simply a project engineering role. It is a portfolio-level technical leadership position responsible for protecting engineering integrity, ensuring technical consistency, managing engineering risk, supporting product deployment, and continuously improving the resilience and performance of the Vantage EMEA platform.

The role combines technical leadership, team development, engineering governance, customer engagement, delivery oversight, and strategic growth within a high-performing engineering environment.

Role Purpose

The Technical Director, Electrical Engineering – Mission Critical Engineering, EMEA is responsible for ensuring the technical quality, robustness, resilience, constructability, operational integrity, and engineering compliance of electrical systems deployed across the EMEA portfolio, aligned with Vantage product standards, engineering governance requirements, customer commitments, and operational objectives.

The role acts as a senior technical authority across complex data centre projects from concept through detailed design, construction, commissioning, and operational handover.

Operating Context

Mission Critical Engineering operates as the technical authority within the EMEA CTO organisation.

The function is responsible for:


Engineering assurance across the data centre lifecycle
Technical governance and standards compliance
Design integrity and engineering consistency
Product and reference design technical validation
Technical risk management and resilience assurance
Cross-functional engineering coordination
Continuous technical improvement and lessons learned integration
Technical leadership and engineering oversight across major mission-critical programmes and strategic deployments


The Technical Director, Electrical Engineering – Mission Critical Engineering, EMEA works closely alongside the Electrical Director, Mission Critical Engineering, EMEA in leading electrical engineering strategy, technical governance, and engineering excellence across the regional portfolio.

While the Technical Director provides senior technical leadership across major mission-critical programmes, engineering assurance activities, customer engagements, and complex project delivery, the Electrical Director maintains overall accountability for electrical engineering discipline leadership across the organisation, including engineering resource management, discipline capability development, technical standards governance, and long-term electrical engineering strategy.

Together, these roles provide aligned technical leadership across engineering delivery, product deployment, resilience assurance, engineering standards, and continuous improvement initiatives throughout the EMEA portfolio.

Within this model:


Product owns platform deployment, product governance, and regional adaptation
Mission Critical Engineering owns technical assurance, engineering integrity, resilience validation, and technical governance
Engineering Delivery and Design Management support execution, coordination, and delivery integration
Construction, Commissioning, and Operations provide deployment feedback and operational learning


The Technical Director, Electrical Engineering must ensure engineering excellence is maintained without creating unnecessary engineering complexity, uncontrolled technical divergence, or project-specific engineering behaviours that undermine platform consistency.

Why This Role Matters

The Technical Director, Electrical Engineering – Mission Critical Engineering, EMEA plays a critical role in protecting the technical integrity of the Vantage platform.

This role ensures that mission-critical electrical systems are designed, validated, deployed, and operated to the highest standards of resilience, performance, safety, and operational reliability.

As Vantage continues transitioning toward increasingly standardised, scalable, and product-led deployment models, this role helps ensure technical excellence is not compromised by speed, growth, or complexity.
