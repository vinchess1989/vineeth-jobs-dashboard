About the Role

As a Principal Engineer focusing on Chemical Mechanical Planarization (CMP), you will be a key technical leader in the Process Module team. You will be responsible for developing, optimizing, and sustaining cutting-edge CMP processes critical to our semiconductor fabrication technology nodes. This role requires deep, hands-on CMP technical expertise, a strategic mindset to drive advanced planarization solutions, and excellent communication skills to collaborate with cross-functional integration teams and external partners.

What you will do:

Technical Expertise & CMP Process Development:

Develop and modify CMP process formulations, methods, and tool controls to meet stringent quality standards.

Analyze, and review technical data and charts to ensure CMP performance, focusing on non-uniformity, defectivity, and removal rate stability.

Establish and maintain Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) and Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA) specific to the CMP module.

Integrate CMP equipment capabilities and material systems to meet process module and technology specifications.

Lead recipe creation, maintenance, and optimization for CMP metrology tools (e.g., film thickness measurement, surface inspection) to ensure accurate process monitoring and improve recipe quality.

Leadership & Collaboration:

Drive Continuous Improvement Plans (CIPs) to enhance process performance and productivity.

Lead and participate in cross-functional teams to achieve organizational goals.

Serve as a technical subject matter expert for CMP, providing guidance and training to junior CMP engineers.

Collaborate with vendors and other internal teams for benchmarking and cross-learning initiatives.

Demonstrate mastery in all aspects of the CMP technical discipline, with group-wide recognition as a senior member of a technical team.

Problem-Solving & Innovation:

Solve complex, novel, and non-recurring technical problems.

Proactively identify and implement significant changes to existing processes and methods.

Contribute to intellectual property by being a contributing inventor on patents related to CMP technology.

Participate in and contribute to conference or journal publications.

Safety & Compliance:

Perform all activities in a safe and responsible manner.

Support all Environmental, Health, Safety & Security requirements.

What you need:

Master’s or Bachelor’s Degree in Electrical / Electronics / Mechanical / Chemical / Materials Science Engineering ore related field.

5 to 8 years of relevant experience in wafer fabrication industry, with a strong focus on CMP.

Good interpersonal and communication skills

Good analytical and project management skills

Organized and systematic in problem solving, familiar with SPC, DOE & FMEA
