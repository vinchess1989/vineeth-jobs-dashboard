Our vision is to transform how the world uses information to enrich life for all.

Micron Technology is a world leader in innovating memory and storage solutions that accelerate the transformation of information into intelligence, inspiring the world to learn, communicate and advance faster than ever.

Key Responsibilities

Design & Implementation


Design digital blocks such as control logic, BIST, and DfT using semicustom methodologies.
Define interface, behavior, and functional requirements for digital blocks.
Participate in logic synthesis, timing constraint development, and Place & Route (P&R).
Support RTL-to-GDS block-level workflow, including placement, CTS, routing, and timing closure.
Implement designs following digital & characterization-friendly design guidelines.


Verification & Validation


Perform functional and gate-level verification, including equivalence checking.
Develop testbenches for behavioral and synthesizable designs.
Collaborate with block owners and integration teams to ensure full-chip integration.
Support silicon bring-up, post-silicon validation and debug.


Flow & Methodology


Support the setup and continuous improvement of semicustom design flows.
Develop and maintain semicustom scripting environments for competitive PPAC across all DRAM products.
Prepare and maintain technical documentation.
Use AI-driven tools to streamline workflows and enhance design efficiency.


Required Qualifications

Education


Bachelor's or Master's degree in Electrical/ Electonics Engineering, Computer Engineering, or equivalent.


Experience


6+ years of experience in RTL design and PnR flow.


EDA Tools Proficiency

Synthesis: Synopsys Fusion Compiler / Design Compiler

STA : Synopsys PrimeTime

Simulation : Cadence Xcelium / NCSim / Synopsys VCS

PnR : Synopsys ICC2

Equivalence Check : Synopsys Formality

Power Analysis: ANSYS RedHawk / Totem (preferred)

Technical Skills


Experience with logic synthesis, STA, gate-level simulations, and P&R flows.
Familiarity with full-custom and semicustom design methodologies.
Understanding of CMOS circuit design fundamentals.
Scripting proficiency in Tcl, Perl, Python or similar languages.
Experience with clocking concepts, synthesis constraining, and multi-corner STA.
Familiarity with Unix/Linux operating systems.
