Who We Are

Applied Materials is a global leader in materials engineering solutions used to produce virtually every new chip and advanced display in the world. We design, build and service cutting-edge equipment that helps our customers manufacture display and semiconductor chips – the brains of devices we use every day. As the foundation of the global electronics industry, Applied enables the exciting technologies that literally connect our world – like AI and IoT. If you want to push the boundaries of materials science and engineering to create next generation technology, join us to deliver material innovation that changes the world.

Key Responsibilities and Performance Indicators


Supplier Technology Strategy & Roadmap
Supplier Engagement & Technical Collaboration
Technology Evaluation & Risk Management
Commercial, Cost & Supply Readiness
Legal, IP & Strategic Partnerships
Cross-functional Leadership & Influence
Supplier technology readiness and roadmap alignment
Cost optimization and sourcing efficiency
Reduction in supply and technology risks
Successful qualification of supplier technologies
Time-to-market improvements
Supplier performance in quality, delivery, and scalability


Skills :


Evaluate supplier capabilities in terms of : Technology readiness level (TRL), Manufacturing maturity, Scalability and long-term


viability


Identify: Technology gaps, Single-source risks, Dependency risks
Act as the primary technical interface for strategic suppliers
Lead Technical Roadmap Meetings (TRMs) to align : Supplier development plans, Milestones, Product requirements
Drive joint development programs from concept to qualification phase.
Collaborate with cross-functional teams: Engineering, Quality & Reliability ,Operations & Manufacturing
Ensure supplier solutions meet requirements for : Performance, Reliability, Manufacturability & Cost
Conduct deep technical assessments of supplier products and processes.
Validate: Design robustness, Quality systems, Process controls
Analyze risks related to: Yield, Reliability, Scaling to volume production
Drive corrective actions and improvement plans with suppliers.
Partner with Supply Chain and Sourcing teams to evaluate: Cost competitiveness, Total Cost of Ownership (TCO), Supplier


capacity and scalability


Assess supplier readiness for : Volume ramp, Stable yield, Long-term supply assurance
Provide technical inputs for: Make vs Buy decisions, Supplier selection & Strategic sourcing
Support development of : Joint Development Agreements (JDAs), Define and protect Intellectual Property (IP), Strategic


investments


Translate complex technical topics into clear inputs for: Business decisions, Executive reviews
Influence stakeholders across: Engineering, Supply Chain, Product Management


Experience:


Bachelor’s /Master's in Mechanical /Electrical Engineering/Materials Science Engineering or Related field
10–15 years of experience in Semiconductor Equipment domain, advanced manufacturing, supplier development, and/or


technology engineering; project management experience or relevant industry certifications preferred.


Experience working with : Global Suppliers, Cross-functional teams, New product development (NPI)
Proficiency working with Excel, Word, PowerPoint skills.


Core Competencies:


Strong strategic thinking and sound decision-making abilities
Proven technical leadership
Effective supplier collaboration and influencing capability
Expertise in risk identification, assessment, and mitigation
Ability to lead and work within cross-functional teams
Drive for innovation and continuous improvement


Requirements:


An Indian citizen with valid passport & ability to travel to Asia, Singapore, China & USA
Ability to travel 20% of the work time
Flexibility to work from home/office environment
As required Flexibility to work in multiple time zones to collaborate with teams across regions
