Description

Invent the future with us.

Ampere is a semiconductor design company for a new era, leading the future of computing with an innovative approach to CPU design focused on high-performance, energy efficient AI compute.

As a pioneer in the new frontier of energy efficient high-performance computing, Ampere is part of the Softbank Group of companies driving sustainable computing for AI, Cloud, and edge applications.

Join us at Ampere and work alongside a passionate and growing team - we’d love to have you apply! 

About the role:

We’re seeking a Senior Principal / Lead Analog Mixed-Signal Design Engineer to drive high‑impact, high‑visibility analog IP for next‑generation AI, cloud, and edge applications. You will own critical power‑management and analog IP from concept to production, lead cross‑functional teams, mentor engineers, and influence roadmap and make/buy decisions.

What you’ll achieve:


Own end‑to‑end design of LDOs, buck/multiphase converters, references, op‑amps/OTAs, comparators, and current/thermal sensors.
Define specs with system architects and AI hardware teams; translate workloads into efficiency, transient response, ripple, PSRR, noise, and reliability targets.
Drive architecture and control strategies, loop compensation, and stability across PVT and load conditions.
Build behavioral models (Verilog‑A/AMS), run AMS co‑sims, corners, and noise analyses.
Guide layout and physical integration, substrate noise isolation, and ESD/latch‑up robustness; partner with SoC PD/package/PDN teams.
Lead silicon bring‑up, test plans, debug/root cause, and close on yield and variation.
Establish best practices and mentor the team; report progress and trade‑offs to leadership.


About you:


Bachelor's degree with 12+ years or Master’s degree with 8+ years of experience inanalog/mixed‑signal design with a strong record of productized IPs /SoCs; 3+ years leading projects or teams.
Deep expertise in power‑management/precision circuits and load‑transient optimization, protection, and telemetry.
Strong command of noise, PSRR, and stability/compensation.
Skilled in variation/reliability (PVT, mismatch, corners/Monte Carlo, aging) and post‑silicon correlation.
Experience in advanced CMOS processes; understanding of package/PDN co‑design.
Proficient with Cadence Virtuoso, Spectre, and scripting (Python/SKILL).
Excellent cross‑functional communication.
