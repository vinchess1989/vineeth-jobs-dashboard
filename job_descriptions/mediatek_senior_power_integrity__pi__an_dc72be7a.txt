Job Description

MediaTek is a global fabless semiconductor industry leader, providing solutions from the edge to the cloud and powering over 2 billion connected devices every year. Established in 1997, our leading-edge technologies keep the world connected and enhance everyday life. At the forefront of innovation, MediaTek drives advancements in transformative technologies such as AI, 5G/6G, and Wi-Fi 8. Our high-performance, power-efficient solutions form the foundation for a smarter, more connected world, enabling devices from smartphones, smart homes, and AI PCs to high-performance computing, automotive, and AI data centers. As a trusted partner to the world's leading brands, we are committed to ensuring access to world-class technology for everyone. Our dedication to accelerating AI underscores our mission to enrich the future of humanity. About the Role: Power integrity is becoming a first-order design constraint at advanced technology nodes. As a Senior Power Integrity (PI) Analysis Engineer, you will own IR-drop sign-off and power grid implementation for high-performance CPU blocks, bridging physical design and system-level power delivery to ensure robust, silicon-proven voltage margins. What You’ll Do: 1. Own block-level IR drop sign-off (static and dynamic) and power grid implementation for CPU blocks. 2. Set up PI analysis environments, including package models and bump/ball map configurations. 3. Define power grid requirements in close collaboration with physical design teams. 4. Drive power grid architecture decisions to meet sign-off criteria across all operating points. 5. Deliver CPM models for SoC-level integration and customer power delivery modeling. 6. Develop, document, and champion advanced PI methodologies through MediaTek’s worldwide PI forum. 7. Collaborate with package, board, and PMIC teams to ensure end-to-end power delivery integrity. Location: San Jose, CA/Austin, TX

Main Requirements and Qualifications


1. Bachelor’s or Master’s degree in Electrical Engineering, Computer Engineering, or a related field.
2. 5+ years of hands-on power integrity experience on high-performance CPU or similar high-speed designs.
3. Deep expertise in static and dynamic IR drop analysis on advanced process nodes (5 nm or below).
4. Experience with hierarchical PI analysis, multi-voltage domains, and multi-clock architectures.
5. Knowledge of multi-OPP sign-off, including resolving IR/timing conflicts driven by congestion and clock variation.
6. Familiarity with low-power design methodologies (CPF/UPF, power gating, DVFS).
7. Proficiency with Cadence Voltus and/or Synopsys RedHawk-SC.
8. Strong scripting skills in Tcl, Python, or Perl.
Proven advanced-node tape-out experience (sub-7 nm required; sub-3 nm a plus).
Nice to Have
1. Power integrity experience with ARM Cortex-A or custom CPU designs.
2. Familiarity with physical design and STA flows (synthesis, place-and-route, timing sign-off).
3. Experience with package and PCB co-simulation for power delivery network analysis.
