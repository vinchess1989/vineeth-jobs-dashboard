Job Description



Role Overview



The Sr Hardware Validation engineer serves as embedded hardware domain expert, driving product and platform design quality, evaluation and validation objectives, and technical execution while collaborating closely with systems, firmware/software, validation, and platform develop teams.



Key Responsibilities



Lead hardware validation planning and execution of embedded products, applications and solutions utilizing a wide portfolio of Renesas products
Drive hardware technical decisions, ensuring robustness, usability and completeness of hardware platforms
Own hardware execution from ideation, validation through qualification and production readiness.
Collaborate closely with systems, firmware/software, validation, and product engineering teams to ensure seamless hardware‑software integration.
Identify and mitigate hardware‑related technical risks, ensuring highest user experience
Planning and execution of new product prototype bring-up, bench-testing, and evaluation.
Support creation and review of technical documentation (Datasheets, User Manuals/Guides, Application Notes etc)
Feedback findings, issues, potential improvements to product development teams
Contribute to reference designs, development platforms, and documentation to enable broad customer adoption.
Act as a technical mentor, influencing best practices and raising the bar for hardware design excellence.


Qualifications


B. Tech/M. Tech in Electrical Engineering, Electronics, Computer Engineering, with at least 5- 7 years of experience
5-7 years of experience in embedded hardware and system design and validation encompassing microcontrollers, power management and analog/mixed-signal products
Strong understanding of SoC architecture, ARM cores (A/M series), peripherals, power/clock/reset, and low-power design techniques.
Experience validating high-speed interfaces (e.g., DDR, USB, PCIe, MIPI, Ethernet) and standard protocols (SPI, I2C, UART, CAN, etc.).
Knowledge of C/C++ or embedded programming for firmware development
Hands-on experience with lab tools (oscilloscopes, logic analyzers, power analyzers, thermal sensors).
Familiarity with validation frameworks, debug utilities (JTAG, Lauterbach, OpenOCD), and issue tracking systems (JIRA, Bugzilla).
Strong leadership, communication, and collaboration skills.
Hands‑on experience with Signal Integrity (SI) and Power Integrity (PI) analysis for high‑speed digital, mixed‑signal, and power‑dense designs.
Applying best practices in schematic design, PCB layout, stack‑up definition, decoupling strategies, and grounding.
Proven experience delivering embedded hardware designs from concept through production.

Preferred qualifications



Experience supporting embedded development platforms or reference designs with broad customer adoption across Industrial IoT, Home/Building Automation and Automotive segments
Execute validation of boot flows, power/clock/reset, memory subsystems, interconnects, and interfaces across stress, corner, and fault scenarios.
Drive system bring‑up on evaluation boards, emulation/FPGA platforms, and silicon validation setups.
Perform hands‑on silicon hardware and root‑cause analysis, working closely with design, DV, firmware, and software teams to drive closure.
Analyse validation data and logs, track issues through structured closure, and contribute to quality metrics.
Mentor junior engineers and provide technical guidance within the validation team.
Excellent verbal and written communication skills, including executive-level messaging and presentations.
Ability to work effectively in a multicultural, cross-functional environment.
