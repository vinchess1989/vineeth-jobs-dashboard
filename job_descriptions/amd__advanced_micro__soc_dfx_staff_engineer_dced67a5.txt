THE ROLE:

We are seeking an experienced SoC DFX Engineer to help define, design, and verify robust Design for Test (DFT) and Design for Debug (DFD) solutions for complex, high‑performance SoCs. In this role, you will collaborate closely with architecture, RTL, firmware, and verification teams to integrate DFX features that enable efficient silicon validation, bring‑up, and debug across the full product lifecycle. This position offers hands‑on technical depth, system‑level problem solving, and the opportunity to influence DFX strategy in advanced processor designs.

  

THE PERSON:  

You are a curious and driven engineer with a strong interest in complex processor architectures and system‑level problem solving. You bring a collaborative mindset and communicate clearly and professionally, enabling you to work effectively with cross‑functional teams distributed across sites and time zones. You are proactive, adaptable, and comfortable navigating ambiguity, with the ability to break down complex technical challenges and drive them to resolution. You take ownership of your work, value constructive feedback, and are eager to learn, continuously improve, and contribute to both technical excellence and a positive team culture.

  

KEY RESPONSIBILITIES:  

Collaborate with architects, hardware engineers, and firmware engineers to understand the new DFX features to be designed and verified.
Work on designing and integrating DFX logic in complex SoCs.
Build test plan documentation, accounting for interactions with other features, the hardware, the firmware, and the software driver use cases 
Estimate the time required to write the new feature tests and any required changes to the test environment 
Build the directed and random verification tests 
Debug test failures to determine the root cause; work with RTL and firmware engineers to resolve design defects and correct any test issues 
Review functional and code coverage metrics – modify or add tests or constrain random tests to meet the coverage requirements 

  

PREFERRED EXPERIENCE:  

Experience with DFT (Design for Test) and DFD (Design for Debug) architecture, design, and verification
Experience with JTAG, LBIST, MBIST, Scan, and ATPG
Familiarity with the Tessent tools suite, including Scan and ATPG
Familiarity with test pattern generation, bring‑up, and debug on ATE
Experience with post‑silicon bring‑up
Experience with IP‑level and ASIC verification
Experience debugging firmware and RTL code using simulation tools
Experience with Verilog, SystemVerilog, SystemC, and C++
Experience automating workflows in a distributed compute environment
Experience with UVM concepts and the SystemVerilog language
Experience with static timing analysis and constraints
Familiarity with ICL/PDL
Scripting language experience: Perl, Ruby, Makefile, and shell preferred
Exposure to leadership or mentorship is an asset

  

ACADEMIC CREDENTIALS:  

Bachelor’s or Master’s degree in Electrical Engineering, Computer Engineering, or a related field.
