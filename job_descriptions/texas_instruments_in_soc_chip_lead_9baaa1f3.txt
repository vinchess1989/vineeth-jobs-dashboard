Job Description

We are at a pivotal moment where technology is rapidly evolving, and we have some exciting opportunities within our MCU organization that place us right at the center of this transformation.

Our current work is focused on developing solutions for high-growth markets such as AI-driven data centers and emerging domains like humanoid systems. These areas are shaping the future of computing, automation, and intelligent systems, and offer a unique chance to work on cutting-edge technologies with real-world impact.

If you are passionate about solving complex technical challenges and want to contribute to innovations in these fast-growing segments, I encourage you to come forward and apply. This is an opportunity to be part of a journey that is not only technically rewarding but also strategically significant.

Looking forward to seeing many of you take this step.

Role Title: SoC Chip Lead / design manager

Role Summary: Own end-to-end delivery of the first T28 industrial ASIC for the MSP M33 Data center business. This role combines full chip ownership with functional team leadership, ensuring first-time-right silicon, scalable architecture, and a strong RTL organization for future platforms.

Key Responsibilities


Own SoC execution from spec → architecture → RTL → Silicon → mass production
Act as single technical owner (Chip Lead) for the first T28 industrial ASIC
Drive PPA, quality, cost, and schedule trade-offs
Lead execution across RTL, DV, DFT, PD and Validation.
Interface with Architecture, IP, FW/SW, Product, Business teams, PE team.
Own pre-silicon sign-off and post-silicon bring-up & validation
Lead and grow the design team
Ensure high-quality micro-architecture, coding, reuse, and integration
Balance hands-on technical leadership with people management


Qualifications

Minimum Qualifications:


Bachelors/Masters in EE or equivalent
12 years experience in complex SoC/ASIC development
Leadership experience in multiple SoC domains.
Strong expertise in: ARM-based systems, interconnects, Low-power design, clock/reset/power architecture
End-to-end SoC lifecycle exposure including RTL → PnR → timing closure
Strong pre- and post-silicon debug skills, Multiple successful tape-outs
Excellent communication and cross-team leadership skills


Preferred


Prior Chip Lead / SoC Lead role
Experience with first-silicon or new-node (T28) programs
Industrial SoC exposure
Post-silicon bring-up and yield learning experience

About Texas Instruments

Texas Instruments Incorporated (Nasdaq: TXN) is a global semiconductor company that designs, manufactures and sells analog and embedded processing chips for markets such as industrial, automotive, data center, personal electronics and communications equipment. At our core, we have a passion to create a better world by making electronics more affordable through semiconductors. This passion is alive today as each generation of innovation builds upon the last to make our technology more reliable, more affordable and lower power, making it possible for semiconductors to go into electronics everywhere.
