General Summary

As a leading technology innovator, Qualcomm pushes the boundaries of what's possible to enable next-generation experiences and drives digital transformation to help create a smarter, connected future for all. As a Qualcomm Hardware Engineer, you will plan, design, optimize, verify, and test electronic systems, bring-up yield, circuits, mechanical systems, Digital/Analog/RF/optical systems, equipment and packaging, test systems, FPGA, and/or DSP systems that launch cutting-edge, world class products. Qualcomm Hardware Engineers collaborate with cross-functional teams to develop solutions and meet performance requirements.

Minimum Qualifications


Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Electrical/Electronics Engineering, Engineering, or related field and 2+ years of Hardware Engineering or related work experience.



OR

Master's degree in Computer Science, Electrical/Electronics Engineering, Engineering, or related field and 1+ year of Hardware Engineering or related work experience.

OR

PhD in Computer Science, Electrical/Electronics Engineering, Engineering, or related field.

Job Description (PDN – Power Integrity Engineer)


Ability to extrapolate and predict traditional electrical behavior using various AI/ML techniques.
Strong in analog and digital design and circuits
Extraction of PDN parameters using industry standard tools.
Good understanding of package and PCB.
Work with package and board design teams to enable analysis and closure of PDN rails.
Design power delivery network for die, package and board. Define bump/BGA patterns for on-die power grid and decoupling capacitance analysis.
Perform PDN design, simulation, analysis, and debug.
Derive PDN design budgets like IR Drops, droops, PMIC requirements.
Conduct power integrity analysis for high performance IPs, and provide feedback for Low Power Chip and System Architecture.
Work with system designers, design verification and physical design teams to define, implement, and verify power delivery.
Good understanding of power, timing, IR drop and issues seen in lower tech nodes.
Participate in development of PDN methodology formulation.
Lab measurements of PDN in frequency and time domain.
Good communication skills and ability to work with cross functional teams.



Requirements


Masters/Phd/Postdoc Candidates with 4-7 years of industry experience in power delivery network design and signal integrity analysis.
Expertise in signal and power integrity with preferred experience in on-die power grid design, decoupling capacitor analysis, and coupling analysis.
Expertise in cost-effective Power Distribution network design for high density/high performance chips.
Measurements skills: VNA, spectrum analyzer, oscilloscope.
Looking for candidates holding Masters/PhD in ECE with 4-7 years experience in signal and power integrity domain with understanding of power delivery networks and system design for high density/high performance chips.



Additional Toolset Experience


Conversant in languages (python/tcl/C), with understanding of software algorithms and data structures.
Cadence APD, Voltus, PowerSi, Ansys HFSS, Q3D, SiWave, HSpice, ADS and Matlab.
