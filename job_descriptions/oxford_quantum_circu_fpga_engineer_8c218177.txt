Bewerben
Speichern
At OQC, we aren't just theorising about the future; we're building it. Born from a philosophy of bold innovation, we've successfully transitioned quantum computing from an academic dream into a commercial reality. The most exciting thing is that we're just getting started.

The Mission

We're looking for an FPGA Engineer to help build the next generation of quantum computing hardware. This role sits at the intersection of advanced electronics, precision timing, and high-performance system design — developing FPGA-based solutions that power critical quantum technologies including QEC and microwave pulse generation.

You'll play a key role in designing reprogrammable, deterministic hardware platforms where performance, latency, and reliability are mission-critical.

The Role

As an FPGA Engineer within our Advanced Product Development Group, you'll design, prototype, test, and implement FPGA-driven hardware systems for cutting-edge quantum computing applications. You'll collaborate across hardware, firmware, software, and R&D teams to deliver scalable, production-ready solutions that push technical boundaries.

What You'll Be Working On


Designing and developing FPGA-based hardware components for quantum computing systems
Building low-latency, precision-timed solutions using Verilog and/or VHDL
Defining timing constraints and driving timing closure for complex FPGA designs
Collaborating with software, R&D, and engineering teams to gather requirements and deliver robust technical solutions
Prototyping, testing, debugging, and iterating on hardware designs to improve system performance
Supporting supplier evaluation, component validation, and inbound quality control activities
Leading technical discussions, whiteboard sessions, and design reviews to share expertise and shape next-generation hardware platforms


What We're Looking For


Experience developing FPGA solutions using Verilog and/or VHDL
Strong understanding of FPGA design flows, timing constraints, and timing closure
Experience building, modifying, and improving existing FPGA architectures
Knowledge of board-level schematics, instrumentation, and hardware test infrastructure
Hands-on experience with laboratory tools and hardware debugging
Ability to clearly communicate FPGA concepts across multidisciplinary engineering teams
Familiarity with Git workflows and CI/CD practices
Degree in Electronics or a related discipline, or equivalent industry experience
Knowledge of ADC/DAC systems and integration


The 'Nice-to-Haves'


Experience with FPGA verification and test methodologies
Background in Digital Signal Processing (DSP)
Experience designing RF, SDR, or high-frequency applications
Experience developing low-latency systems within fintech or similarly performance-critical environments
Experience with Altera FPGA platforms
Electronics troubleshooting, PCB design, schematic capture, or board layout experience


Why Join OQC

You will join a world-class team at the forefront of the next computational era. We offer a culture of bold innovation, the chance to work with unique lab infrastructure, and the opportunity to see your work redefine the limits of computation.

Learn more about our benefits and positive work culture here: https://oqc.tech/company/careers-at-oqc/
