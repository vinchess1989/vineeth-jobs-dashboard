The Role

As a member of the Computing & Graphics (CG) SoC Architecture team, you will develop and design silicon for client, discrete graphics, handheld gaming, gaming consoles, and related platforms. In this role, you will work with the lead SoC architect to define future architectures and contribute to product specifications, micro-architecture specifications, performance, and power trade-offs. The role will work from early concept development through the development phases to final production.

THE PERSON: 

In this role, you will collaborate with a network of expert architects and designers around the world where collaboration and communication skills are critical to the success of this role, and excellent organization skills are essential. You will approach sophisticated problems with a firm methodology and problem-solving technique and conduct experiments and analysis to formulate recommendations and next steps for other colleagues and SOC and IP design teams located in across multiple time zones and geographies. You will formally present your findings to co-workers, teams, and customer engineering teams.

Key Responsibilities


Work on next-generation SOC debug solutions - enabling secure, robust bring-up and post-silicon debug
Collaborate with technology, silicon validation, and product engineering teams to identify DFX strategy for both functional operating mode and manufacturing test mode
Analyze DFX (Design-for-Test, Design-for-Debug) requirements with various AMD Engineering teams to align on use cases/needs and drive end-to-end flow definition
Work with architects and SOC design team to define and document memory test architecture strategy, including:
Repair analysis and fuse programming
Power-aware and low-power test modes
Work with architects and SOC design team to define SMS strategy, including:
SOC reset, clock, and power domains
JTAG / test access mechanisms
Firmware-controlled repair and status reporting
Work with architects and SOC design team to define scan strategy


Preferred Experience


Minimum 7 years relevant experience in SOC design/DFX
Solid understanding of SoC DFX construction in complex SOC designs
Knowledge of DFT architectures including scan, ATPG, MBIST, JTAG/boundary scan and DFX features
Knowledge of Synopsys STAR memory system (SMS) solutions for embedded memories (SRAM, register, files, ROM, etc.)
Understanding of SOC clocking, reset, and power architectures
Strong problem solving, organizational and communication skills, and ability to work in a dynamic and diverse environment
Detailed thinking skills. Ability deal with novel problems from different perspectives and from different levels of abstraction
Ability to analyze and understand sophisticated workflows and processes, and develop innovative ways in streamlining and automating
Proficiency in scripting languages (Perl, TCL, Python) is highly preferred



Academic Credentials


Bachelors or Masters degree in computer engineering/Electrical Engineering
