Anord Mardix, a Flex company, is a global leader in critical power solutions. With facilities around the globe, Anord Mardix leads in critical power infrastructure, supporting a myriad of industries, from financial institutions to data centres. At Anord Mardix, we welcome people of all backgrounds. We develop skill sets, encourage curiosity, and enable empowerment – we turn jobs into careers. If this sounds interesting, we’d like to meet you.

To support our extraordinary teams who build great products and contribute to our growth, we’re looking to add a Engineering Director - EMEA located in one of our location in EMEA region (UK, Poland or Ireland).

Reporting to the Senior Director – Global Engineering, the Engineering Director - EMEA role involves providing strategic, technical, and operational leadership across regional engineering teams. The role focuses on developing and executing the EMEA Engineering strategy, ensuring strong delivery performance, and driving alignment with global engineering objectives while supporting the design and delivery of engineered-to-order electrical and mechanical solutions for mission-critical environments.

What a typical day looks like:


Developing and executing the EMEA Engineering strategy aligned with global objectives and business growth plans
Leading multiple Design & Engineering teams across the region through Heads of Engineering
Overseeing the delivery of complex ETO solutions, ensuring projects are completed on time, within budget, and to quality standards
Driving KPI-based performance management, reporting progress, risks, and improvements to global leadership
Establishing and embedding daily management systems to ensure execution discipline and accountability
Leading continuous improvement initiatives using Lean engineering principles
Collaborating closely with Operations, Manufacturing Engineering, Supply Chain, and Quality to ensure manufacturable and compliant solutions
Leading design reviews, programme governance, and technical decision-making across the region
Building engineering capability through coaching, leadership development, and workforce planning


The experience we’re looking to add to our team:


Strong expertise in BIM, electrical or mechanical engineering, ideally within the critical power or similar industry
Proven experience in leading multi-site, multidisciplinary engineering organisations
Solid understanding of engineered-to-order (ETO) environments and manufacturing-led design
Experience in implementing KPI-driven performance management and governance models
Knowledge of Lean and continuous improvement methodologies applied within engineering
Ability to manage complex technical challenges and make data-driven decisions
Excellent communication and stakeholder management skills, with the ability to influence senior leadership
Demonstrated leadership capability in coaching and developing senior engineering teams
Bachelor’s degree in a relevant field; Master’s or MBA is a plus
Typically 8+ years of leadership experience in engineering environments
