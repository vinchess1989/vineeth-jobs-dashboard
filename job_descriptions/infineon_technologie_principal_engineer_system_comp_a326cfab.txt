Are you in?

Your Role

Key responsibilities in your new role

As a Principal Engineer System Compute, you will define compute architectures for automotive MCUs/MPUs and lead HW/SW co-design to achieve high performance and determinism. You will drive memory and interconnect strategy, performance modeling and optimization, and provide technical leadership across the development lifecycle.


Define compute subsystem architecture for automotive MCUs/MPUs: CPU cores, DSP/vector, and AI accelerators
Translate system requirements into specs and PPA/latency/determinism targets; drive architectural trade-offs
Lead HW/SW co-design with compiler, runtime, firmware, and ML teams to optimize kernels and data movement
Architect memory hierarchy, DMA, interconnect/coherency, and QoS to meet real-time deadlines
Build/guide performance modeling and workload characterization (control, audio, vision, edge inference)
Provide technical leadership across development flow: design reviews, mentoring, roadmap/IP strategy, customer/partner


Your Profile

Qualifications And Skills To Help You Succeed


A master’s degree in Electrical Engineering, Computer Engineering, Computer Science, or related field (advanced degree preferred for this level)
At least 6 years in CPU/DSP/NPU architecture/microarchitecture for embedded/edge SoCs; principal-level leadership
Strong parallel compute expertise (SIMD/vector, multi-core scaling, accelerator integration) and deep understanding of memory systems/interconnects (cache/scratchpad, DMA, coherency, NoC, QoS)
Hands-on capability with modeling/tools (e.g., Python, C/C++, spreadsheets/analytical models, perf simulators, trace tooling); ability to define new methodologies when needed
Familiarity with real-time determinism plus safety/security requirements (isolation, ECC, functional safety concepts)
Great architecture specification, stakeholder alignment, and mentoring skills
Fluent English skills, German is a plus
