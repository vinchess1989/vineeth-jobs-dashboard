#WeAreIn for jobs that impact everyone's life. Join the thinkers, builders, and problemsolvers behind tomorrow’s technology. As a Senior Engineer Analog Verification Methodology on our Research & Development team, you'll have the opportunity to merge creativity with your technical expertise by shaping the future of technology, driving groundbreaking projects, and bringing new ideas to life. Are you in?

Your Role

The performance of large analog and mixed-signal ICs relies heavily on a robust and optimized power network. In this role, you will take ownership of analyzing and enhancing these networks, with a special focus on Electromigration (EM) and Voltage Drop (IR-drop) to ensure reliability and efficiency.

Key Responsibilities In Your New Role


Perform advanced Electromigration and Voltage Drop Analysis on analog/mixed-signal IPs and subsystems
Collaborate closely with Design and Layout teams to interpret results and implement improvements
Drive innovation by proactively improve the methodology and design flow together with the methodology group


Your Profile

You are a detail-oriented engineer with a collaborative mindset, who thrives on tackling complex challenges and sharing expertise across teams. With a solid background in SoC physical design and a passion for building innovative, scalable chip layouts, you are ready to shape the future of reliable semiconductor design.

Qualifications And Skills To Help You Succeed


A Bachelor or Master degree in Electronic Engineering or similar field of studies
1-3 years of experience in the area of analog semiconductor design / verification / layout
A foundation in layout / design / analog verification and Cadence design environment
Knowledge of Electromigration and Voltage Drop Analysis (e. g. Synopsys/Ansys Totem/Redhawk or Cadence Voltus-XFI) is a plus
Good Unix & scripting skills (e.g. Python, Shell programming)
An independent working style with strong communication skills
Full working proficiency in English; German is a plus


The position is limited to 12 months. This position is subject to the collective agreement for workers and employees in the electrical and electronics industry, at least employment group F-G (https://www.feei.at/leistungen/informations-service/mindestlohne-und-gehalter). The monthly salary is paid 14 times p.a. A higher payment is negotiable depending on your expertise and skills. Furthermore, Infineon offers additional employee benefits.
