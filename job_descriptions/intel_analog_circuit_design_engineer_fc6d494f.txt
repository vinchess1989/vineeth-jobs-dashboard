Job Description:

The Role and Impact: As an Analog Circuit Design Engineer, you will be at the forefront of cutting-edge technology, transforming innovative concepts into high-performance analog circuits. Your expertise will drive the development of advanced process nodes for analog and mixed-signal IPs, enabling Intel's next-generation products to achieve exceptional performance and efficiency. You will collaborate with cross-functional teams to design, simulate, and verify circuits that optimize power, performance, area, and yield goals. This role presents an exciting opportunity to shape the future of analog design, tackle complex challenges, and contribute to Intel's mission of delivering world-class solutions. Key Responsibilities: - Design, develop, and implement analog circuits for advanced process nodes focused on high-speed 112/224Gbps SerDes Design, including TX, RX and PHY clocking circuits. - Perform circuit design, parameter extraction, and analog behavior modeling to meet circuit and block microarchitecture specifications. - Create and execute test plans to validate functionality, reliability, and performance of designs, ensuring compliance with specifications. - Optimize circuits for power, performance, area, timing, and leakage while addressing trade-offs in circuit design. - Collaborate with architecture and layout teams to design circuits that deliver robust functionality and superior electrical capabilities. - Conduct detailed validation, including post-silicon testing and debug, and support high-volume production readiness. - Utilize industry-standard tools such as Cadence Virtuoso, SPICE simulators, and extraction tools for design and verification. - Continuously improve design methodologies and contribute to enhancing processes for greater efficiency and innovation. - Drive technical discussions, report progress, and resolve performance and design issues through collaboration with cross-functional teams.

Qualifications

Minimum Qualifications: - Bachelor's or Master's degree in Electrical Engineering or a related specialized field. - Minimum 6 years of hands-on experience with a Bachelor's degree or 4 years with a Master's degree in analog circuit design. - Proficiency in designing Analog/Mixed-signal circuits for 224Gbps/112Gbps SerDes PHY. - Demonstrated capability in using EDA tools such as Cadence Virtuoso, SPICE simulators, and extraction tools like DRC, LVS, and post-layout verification. - Expertise in CMOS technologies and I/O fundamentals. - Experience with circuit simulation methodologies and tools. - Ability to develop circuits optimized for performance, power, area, and yield. Preferred Qualifications: - Strong fundamentals in circuit trade-offs, including power optimization and reliability-aware design. - Experience in post-silicon validation and debugging of pre/post-silicon issues. - Familiarity with scripting languages such as Python, Perl, or TCL for design automation. - Knowledge of industry standards and compliance requirements related to high-speed I/O interfaces. - Proven ability to work in fast-paced environments, solve complex technical challenges, and lead innovative design initiatives. Be part of a team that transforms ideas into reality and shapes the future of analog and mixed-signal design. Apply today to join Intel and help drive innovation that impacts the world.
