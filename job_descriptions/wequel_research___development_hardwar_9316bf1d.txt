Research & Development Hardware Engineer – Innovation UnitLocation: Västerås (Hybrid 50%)

Role OverviewWe are looking for an Advanced R&D Hardware Consultant to work in a highly specialized research and development environment focused on next-generation power electronics solutions.

In this role, you will operate in the interface between research (PhD-level innovation) and product development for mass production, taking technical ownership of future products based on silicon carbide (SiC) semiconductor technology.

You will act as the technical owner and project driver for your assigned product within an agile setup, with a high degree of autonomy and responsibility.

Key Responsibilities

Hardware Development & Design


Design and develop advanced power electronics hardware for products
Perform PCB design and layout using Altium Designer
Integrate electronics into mechanical enclosures, including thermal design and forced convection solutions
Collaborate closely with PhD researchers and innovation specialists to mature concepts into producible designs


Laboratory Work & Testing


Conduct hands-on laboratory testing and verification of prototypes
Work with 400 VAC and higher voltage systems (3-phase) in R&D laboratories
Follow ECAP Level 3 procedures, including controlled energizing/de-energizing of lab equipment
Validate designs through structured testing, measurements, and documentation


Verification & Simulation


Support thermal and electrical validation activities
Contribute to or perform CFD and thermal simulations (e.g., Ansys CFD) where applicable
Analyze test data and iterate designs based on findings


Agile Product Ownership


Act as project manager for your own product within an agile hardware chapter
Drive planning, execution, and delivery of development activities
Coordinate work across multiple agile teams working in parallel on different product tracks
Interface with global units and suppliers


Requirements

Mandatory


PhD in Electrical Engineering, Power Electronics, or a closely related field, or strong industrial R&D background with documented research-level expertise.
Minimum 2 years of hands-on laboratory experience working with 400 VAC or higher voltages
Proven ability to work independently in a laboratory environment (ECAP Level 3 eligibility)
Strong background in power electronics hardware design
Practical experience with Altium Designer for PCB design and layout
Experience with forced convection and thermal management in power electronics systems
Experience working in production-near environments, with understanding of industrialization and manufacturability


Working Style & Collaboration


Self-driven and comfortable taking full technical responsibility
Strong communication skills and ability to collaborate across R&D, engineering, and manufacturing
Experience working in international, agile development environments


Work Arrangement


Hybrid role in Västerås with up to 50% remote work.
100% on-site presence required during critical laboratory and testing periods
Occasional international travel (e.g., Bergamo, Italy or North Carolina, USA)


Why This RoleThis role offers a unique opportunity to work at the forefront of power electronics innovation, with direct influence on the future product portfolio. You will have significant autonomy, access to advanced R&D facilities, and the chance to bring breakthrough technologies from concept to future industrial products.
