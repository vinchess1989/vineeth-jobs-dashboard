Hae 
Tallenna
ReOrbit is looking for a Senior EMI/EMC Engineer to take ownership of electromagnetic compatibility across our next generation satellite platforms. You will work at the intersection of hardware, RF, power and avionics, ensuring spacecraft systems operate reliably in demanding electromagnetic environments. This is a hands on role focused on real hardware, real testing and full system responsibility from design to validation.

This is an exciting opportunity to join a fast-growing company and work on next-generation satellite platforms where EMC is mission-critical from day one.

Responsibilities


Own end to end spacecraft EMC strategy, requirements and verification
Define EMC requirements across avionics, power, AOCS, RF and payload systems
Plan and execute full EMC test campaigns at spacecraft level
Drive grounding, bonding, shielding, and return path design
Design and review filtering, cable harness, and transient protection approaches
Perform conducted and radiated emissions and susceptibility analysis
Define and manage EMC budgets and power quality constraints
Execute hands on EMC testing (conducted/radiated emissions & susceptibility, ESD, transients)
Debug issues in lab using oscilloscopes, probes, LISNs, antennas, and spectrum analyzers
Work cross functionally with avionics, power, RF, AOCS, mechanical, AIT, and software teams
Identify and mitigate EMC induced failure modes at system level
Own final EMC readiness and sign off before flight


Skills And Requirements


10+ years of EMC engineering experience in spacecraft or aerospace systems
Strong hands on experience with emissions and susceptibility testing
Familiarity with ECSS-E-ST-20-07, MIL-STD-461, or DO-160 standards
Experience working across multiple subsystems (avionics, power, RF, AOCS)
Proven ability to diagnose and resolve EMC issues on real hardware
Experience with simulation tools such as ADS, CST, or HFSS
Proficiency in MATLAB or Python for analysis and modelling
Hands on experience with EMI/EMC/RF lab equipment
Comfortable working in integrated hardware/software environments


What does ReOrbit do?

ReOrbit builds the foundation for the next generation of space applications by transforming satellites into truly intelligent, interconnected platforms, defined by software. Our satellite design philosophy revolves around the idea that satellites must network, communicate with each other and thus improve mission efficiency. How can this be achieved? Thanks to a flexible software-first architecture, where all these aspects are dealt with upfront in the mission concept, as integrated functionalities.

ReOrbit’s top-notch team of software engineers and system architects develop in-house software, procure hardware from the best-in-class suppliers, and seamlessly integrate the components to create flexible and secure satellites. Today, we are a rapidly growing company building a very strong multidisciplinary team to support our activities. We are headquartered in Helsinki, Finland with a subsidiaries in Bristol, UK and Bariloche, Argentina.
